Alternative Energies

Alternative energy sources are sources of energy that do not deplete the environment with usage (Oxford Dictionary). These renewables, including sunlight and wind, are plentiful and all around us, meaning they replenish themselves. Generating energy from alternative sources creates far lower emissions than burning fossil fuels, as most renewables are "green" and have significantly lower emissions. Transitioning from fossil fuels, the major pollutant and emitter of fossil fuels, to renewable energy is key to addressing the climate crisis. Some examples of renewable energies include solar, wind, geothermal, hydroelectric, ocean energy, biomass, nuclear, etc.

Per Person Statistics

According to a University of Michigan study, the average daily energy consumption per person is 12 kilowatt-hours. This comes out to 2.5 gallons of oil, and 8.86 pounds of coal daily. However, most of this fuel is not alternative as renewable energy only accounts for 12% of the US energy consumption while nuclear power is only 8% (EIA).
